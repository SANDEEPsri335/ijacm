IJACM Peer Review Process & Policy

Ensuring academic integrity and quality through a transparent, rigorous review process

Overview

International Journal of Advanced Computing and Mechanical Systems (IJACM) follows a rigorous single-blind peer review process to ensure the quality, originality, and integrity of all published work. To maintain the highest standards of academic integrity, the identities of both the authors and the reviewers are concealed throughout the evaluation.

Review Stages & Quality Standards

Initial Submission & Screening

Manuscript assessed by Editor-in-Chief for scope alignment, formatting, and basic scientific soundness.

Expert Assignment

Manuscripts assigned to minimum two independent subject matter experts based on expertise.

Reviewer Evaluation

Reviewers assess technical merit, methodology, and clarity, providing detailed feedback.

Editorial Decision

Editor-in-Chief reviews reports and decides: Accept, Minor Revision, Major Revision, or Reject.

Final Verification

Revised manuscripts sent back to reviewers to ensure all concerns were addressed.

Evaluation Criteria

Reviewers provide feedback based on the following pillars:

Originality

Does the work provide a new contribution to the field?

Technical Rigor

Is the methodology sound and are results supported by data?

Clarity

Is the manuscript well-written, organized, and easy to follow?

Relevance

Does the research address current challenges or future trends?

Ethical Guidelines for Reviewers

Reviewer Ethics

Conflict of Interest

Reviewers must recuse themselves if they have a relationship with authors that could bias judgment.

Confidentiality

Manuscripts are treated as confidential. Reviewers may not share or use data before publication.

Constructive Feedback

Reviewers should be "critical but kind," providing specific suggestions to help authors improve.
